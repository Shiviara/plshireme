EcoBites

Overview

Designing a landing page for a fake company EcoBites which target audience was generated by AI. During this case we tried out various ways of using AI in the design process to see how useful it actually can be.

Time

August 2023

4 days

Team

4 people + AI

Focus area

Landing page design, use of AI, sustainable web design

Key methods

Sprint, crazy 8, wireframing, prototyping

Background

The design sprint’s focus was using AI in the process, but it was up to us to what extend we want to use it. Our task was to create a mock-up of a landing page for a digital product (app or saas) for an AI generated target group. We could chose the audience from a list generated by AI, my team decided on Eco-Conscious Advocates.

Their description was:
Champions of sustainability, choosing eco-friendly products and supporting green initiatives. Their purchases reflect values, promoting eco-awareness. Dedicated to a greener future through informed choices.

My Role

  • Co-ideating the product idea
  • Creating low-fi wireframe of the landing page
  • Co-creating the high-fi wireframe of the landing page
  • Writing some of the text with help of AI

Ideate

Product Idea

We needed to come up with an app or a saas for our target group. We asked chatGPT for suggestions, the ones that got our attention were:

  • Green Product Scanner App - An app that allows users to scan product barcodes to quickly assess the environmental impact of items they're considering purchasing. It could provide information on the product's carbon footprint, packaging materials, and certifications.
  • Community Eco-Action Platform - A social app that connects like-minded Eco-Conscious Advocates, enabling them to share tips, success stories, and local eco-initiatives. It could also facilitate group activities and events.
  • Sustainable Food Choices App - An app that provides information about the environmental impact of different foods, helping users make informed choices about what they eat. It could also suggest plant-based recipes and local farmers' markets.
  • Carbon Offsetting App - A platform that calculates users' carbon footprints and offers options to offset their emissions by supporting verified carbon offset projects.
  • Plant-Based Restaurant Guide - An app that helps users find restaurants and eateries that prioritize plant-based, locally sourced, and organic meals. Users can rate and review dishes, share photos, and discover new sustainable dining options. The app could also include a feature that calculates the carbon savings achieved by choosing plant-based meals over animal-based ones.

After discussing them we formed our idea of zero-waste cookbook app, which helps you use-up your food scraps and find recipes for things that are in season for you based on your location.

Features

When we finally decided what kind of app we want to promote with our landing page, we needed to decide its features. First, we asked chatGPT for help and then we formed more structured list based on it

Because of the sprint nature of the project we decided to minimise the amount of features to:

  • Creating and sharing recipes
  • Suggestions of replacements for ingredients to seasonal ones
  • Recipe category for food scraps

Develop

Low-fi wireframe

As a first step of the development we tried to get some inspiration from images generated by Adobe Firefly and Midjourney. After that we started sketching using crazy 8 method, then we decided on the idea we like the most and we looked at other landing pages to get inspiration. After that we created low-fi wireframe of the website

Color palette

We tried using chatGPT to generate color palette but it was bad...

At the end we chose a colorful and vibrant palette to give casual and approachable vibe, because being eco-friendly should be a everyday thing. The color palette resembles watermelon, which we also choose to make our logo, because entirety of it is edible, so it’s a zero-waste food. We also decided to continue the watermelon theme by coloring sections of the website in a way to form watermelon.

Sustainable Web Design

To make the landing page represent the values of EcoBites (sustainability, fighting food-waste etc) better, we decided to look into sustainable web design. We did desk research about it and we also asked chatGPT how can we use it for EcoBites.

At the end we implemented:

  • Minimalistic design
  • No videos
  • Minimal amount of images
  • Usage of SVGs
